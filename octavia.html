An Introduction to Octavia Butler
Octavia Butler is one such woman in Afrofuturism that has come to heavily shape the movement and embody the divine feminine principle. Born in Pasadena, CA, she he frequently spent time at the Pasadena Public library as a child and became interested in science fiction and fantasy stories by writers like Theodore Sturgeon, Ursula K. Le Guin, and Zenna Henderson. While attending Pasadena City College, she entered writing competitions and worked at night to support herself. She later enrolled at California State University in Los Angeles. One of the defining moments of her early writing career was the Clarion Workshop for sci-fi writers, where she first felt seen, was able to sell her work, and met other Black writers that would become mentors and friends. 
Some of her most notable novels include Kindred, Parable of the Sower, Wildseed, and Fledgling, all of which contain unique explorations of science, gender, race, and spirituality. Described as a “writer’s writer,” Butler provided a blueprint for how women of color could operate in alternate worlds and skewed realities. She set the stage for stories about multidimensional Black women in complex worlds who were vulnerable, stood with courage in the face of tumultuous circumstances, and took it upon themselves to enlighten those around them with innovative solutions. She validated the voices of other writers who wanted to explore this combination of themes in their work, such as N.K. Jemisin and Nnedi Okorafor.
